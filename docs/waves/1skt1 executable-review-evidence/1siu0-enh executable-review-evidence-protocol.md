## Rationale

Wavefoundry's reviewer seeds already teach strong defect classes: branch completeness, re-entrant safety, cache-key completeness, idempotence, failure paths, evidence grounding, adversarial challenge, and council falsification. In practice, however, those rules are usually applied as code-reading questions. They do not consistently require the reviewer to turn a material claim into an executable counterexample, drive that counterexample through the public surface, prove the regression test reaches the defective path, or independently re-run the exact attack after repair.

The `1seav` and `1ro44` reviews showed the difference. Executed state-transition and interleaving probes found reset-after-gate races, initial-absence ABA, between-probe TOCTOU, dirty-epoch lockout, cross-process cache incoherence, malformed successful subprocess streams, skipped/vacuous tests, and live-process drift that source inspection and green suites had missed. The same reviews also exposed the opposite failure mode: adversarial review can overstate a local symlink read as a critical security boundary or mistake an optional capability's absence (for example, a project without Git) for provider corruption. The framework needs a shared, proportional evidence protocol that produces more real findings and fewer speculative ones across every installed project.

Those reviews exposed a second process defect: the framework validates findings and then jumps directly to a small-fix-versus-follow-on heuristic. It does not require a product judgment about whether the observation is actionable, whether the proposed repair is proportionate, or whether another unrestricted review round is justified. A technically valid adjacent finding can therefore expand into another fix, another council, and another adjacent finding indefinitely. The protocol must make findings converge: every validated observation receives an explicit action synthesis, actionable bounded work is completed now, rejected work is closed rather than laundered into a vague backlog, and repair review stays bounded to the repaired claim unless the repair changes a load-bearing boundary.

14. Add a canonical **Finding Actionability and Review Convergence Gate** after deduplication and evidence collection, before fix scheduling. It is an ordered state machine, not a moderator-selected label:

    1. `validation_status in {invalid, conforming}` → `not_issue`. A required AC/public-contract violation is `real`, not `conforming`; absence of observed harm alone never overrides a required contract.
    2. `validation_status == real` and derived `action_required == true` → `do_now`. The exact predicate is: `action_required == true` iff `validation_status == real` and at least one holds: (a) `contract_relevance in {required_ac, public_contract}`; (b) `introduced_or_worsened_by_wave == true` and `supported_reachability == true`; (c) `supported_reachability == true`, `observable_impact in {material, critical}`, and `containment in {detect_only, none, unverified}`; or (d) `supported_reachability == true`, `attacker_reachability == true`, `authority_delta in {material, critical}`, and `containment != preventive`. Fix difficulty or a harmful proposed repair cannot downgrade the underlying proposition—the bad repair is rejected separately and the unresolved finding is synthesized again.
    3. Remaining `real` propositions with `optional_value == positive`, `repair_scope_bounded == true`, `repair_safety == safe`, `scope_relation == admitted`, and `benefit_vs_fix_risk == greater` → `maybe_later`.
    4. All remaining `real` propositions → `dont_do_later`.

An unsupported payload delivered through a supported public entry point remains supported attacker reachability.

Lack of authorization never converts a candidate to `dont_do_later` or `not_issue`.

## Scope

**Problem statement:** Review agents know which bug classes to consider but are not consistently required to falsify claims with executable public-path evidence, validate test non-vacuity, or independently re-run repaired attacks. This permits green suites and multi-seat councils to approve mechanisms that were never actually exercised, while unconstrained adversarial review can also overstate speculative or unreachable risks.

**In scope:**

- Canonical shared executable-evidence protocol and finding/approval evidence expectations.
- State-transition, interleaving, repetition, partial-failure, and recovery review guidance.
- Public-path verification and universal-claim census requirements.
- QA test-integrity requirements, including pre-fix failure proof and unintended-skip detection.
- Exact-reproduction plus adjacent-control re-verification after repairs.
- Review-depth scaling and proportionality/security-reachability calibration.
- Four-way finding synthesis, actionable-work-now semantics, explicit rejection without backlog laundering, and bounded repair-review convergence.
- Prospective executable-evidence companion records with phase, safety, census, and independence metadata.
- Safe local probing and explicit authority limits for destructive/external operations.
- Seed and rendered-surface propagation for fresh installs, upgrades, and self-hosting.
- Deterministic framework-owned-section reconciliation plus focused propagation/schema/conformance tests.

**Out of scope:**

- A general-purpose property-testing, model-checking, or concurrency-testing framework.
- Mandatory mutation testing for every change; focused pre-fix proof is sufficient.
- Requiring live external services or network access for review evidence.
- Making every low-risk/docs-only review run a full transition matrix.
- Changing which specialist lanes own security, architecture, performance, or QA verdicts.
- Automatically approving findings or closure based only on structured fields.
- Automatically creating follow-on plans for every valid observation or preserving vague “could be follow-on” debt without a promotion trigger.
- Rewriting historical wave review records to match the new protocol.

## Risks


| Risk | Mitigation |
| ---- | ---------- |
| Review cost grows until teams bypass it | Depth scaling, highest-risk-probe minimum, admitted-scope bound, and explicit N/A rationale. |
| Agents cargo-cult phrases without executing probes | Public-path expected/observed evidence and propagation fixtures; QA validates non-vacuity. |
| Reviewers overstate speculative risks | Capability/failure taxonomy plus reachability, observable impact, and authority-delta severity guard. |
| Protocol drifts across role docs and projects | One canonical shared seed, short carrier references, install/upgrade/render tests. |
| Existing review records fail new parsing rules | Make the structured companion record prospective and additive; historical finding/approval syntax remains valid and is never rewritten. |
| Public-path probes broaden authority or cause destructive/external effects | Canonical safety disposition, disposable/read-only defaults, explicit current authorization for risky effects, honest unverified fallback. |
| A target repository presents a pre-existing/static platform-root or framework-carrier symlink outside its root | Preflight every selected platform root plus every registered/native agent destination before cleanup or the first write; direct render, setup, and upgrade common-ancestor sentinels prove no external mutation for the repository state presented to the command. Concurrent local namespace substitution after preflight is outside the supported threat model; race-resistant cross-platform filesystem traversal is intentionally rejected without field evidence. |
| Presence tests are mislabeled as reviewer-behavior proof | Separate contract-presence tests from bounded delivery dogfooding and schema/refusal conformance cases. |
| Each repaired finding produces another unrestricted adjacent audit | Four-way synthesis, focused recheck for non-blocking work, full-council escalation only for load-bearing repairs, and a mandatory convergence checkpoint after two repair cycles. |
